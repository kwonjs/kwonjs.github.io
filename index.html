A bit about me~

Image description: On the left, an illustration of a Korean American genderqueer individual with slightly tan skin, smiling and tilting their head slightly to the right, is pictured. They have long black hair tied up in a ponytail, piercings, slightly lifted dark eyebrows and dark brown eyes. They are wearing gold eyeshadow, black eyeliner, purple spectacles and a purple tank top.

Hi y'all! I'm Jenny and I use they/she pronouns. I am currently a third-year student majoring in Public Health at UC Berkeley and triple-minoring in Journalism, Data Science and Global Poverty & Practice. My passions lie in art; disability justice; the prison-military industrial complex (and how academia plays a role in this); LGBTQIA+ identity and rights, AANHPI (Asian American Native Hawaiian Pacific Islander) justice; big tech, ethics and policy and the intersections of all of these topics! I hope to serve local communities either as an investigative data journalist reporting on health-related issues or as an epidemiologist in the future.
