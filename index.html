About me~



Hi y'all! I'm Jenny, I use they/them pronouns and I am based in the ‚õÖ foggy city of San Francisco ‚õÖ. I am a graphics reporter (read: front-end developer, designer, data reporter, occasional illustrator) for The San Francisco Chronicle.

Prior to working at The Chronicle, I interned at The Seattle Times as a graphics intern during Summer 2022 and the San Francisco Public Press during Summer 2021 as a data journalism intern, creating graphics and helping co-report/research stories related to health and housing. While my first official data journalism stint (and brief foray into feature writing and copy editing) was in Spring 2021 with InfoNile, a geodata journalism initiative based in Kampala, Uganda, I did exploratory data analysis and research on child welfare-related data for the Investigative Reporting Program (UC Berkeley School of Journalism) and open source intelligence (OSINT) reserach on wildfire effects and health worker discrimination for the Human Rights Center (UC Berkeley School of Law). From 2022 through 2024, I volunteered and served as a volunteer lead at the Asian American Journalism Association Conference.

I graduated from UC Berkeley, where I majored in Public Health and minored in Journalism, Data Science and Global Poverty & Practice. My passions lie in art; disability justice; the prison-military industrial complex (and how academia plays a role in this); LGBTQIA+ identity and rights, AANHPI (Asian American Native Hawaiian Pacific Islander) justice; big tech, ethics and policy and the intersections of all of these topics.
